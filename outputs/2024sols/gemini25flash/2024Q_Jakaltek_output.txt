**Q4. Explain the structure of the Jakaltek numbering system.**

The Jakaltek numbering system is vigesimal (base-20). Numbers are typically constructed in an additive manner: `[Units] + [Multiplier] * 20`.

*   **Units (1-19):** Basic units have specific names, often ending in `-eb'` (e.g., `hune'`=1, `kab'eb'`=2, `b'alunheb'`=4, `hujeb'`=9, `kanheb'`=16). Some units for 11-13 (and potentially higher) are formed by adding a base number to 10 (e.g., `lahun`=10, `hunlahun`=1+10=11, `oxlahun`=3+10=13). There are instances where a word like `wajlahunheb'` (7) represents a specific unit despite containing `lahun`.
*   **Scores (Multiples of 20):** Multiples of 20 are formed by a multiplier followed by `k'al` (e.g., `hunk'al`=1*20=20, `oxk'al`=3*20=60, `hunlahunhk'al`=11*20=220).
*   **Compound Numbers:** For numbers greater than 19, the structure is generally `[Units] [Multiplier for Scores]k'al`, which translates to `Units + (Multiplier * 20)`. The `s` prefix often precedes the multiplier for scores (e.g., `slahunhk'al` for 9*20). There might be variations in unit representation within compound numbers (e.g., `hujeb'` meaning 14 in J6, while its base meaning is 9), suggesting context-dependent interpretations for units or specific names for certain numerical compounds.
