## L3: What I've figured out about Coptic

Based on the analysis:

1. **Word order**: Coptic generally follows a noun-adjective/modifier order, with possessives and numbers following the noun.

2. **Articles**: 
   - **p-** = the (masculine singular)
   - **t-** = the (feminine singular)
   - **ou-** = a/an (indefinite)
   - **hen-** = some

3. **Genitive construction**: Uses **nte** or **n-/m-** to indicate possession ("of")

4. **Possessive prefixes** attach to nouns:
   - pa- = my
   - pek-/tek- = your(sg) (m/f)
   - pef-/tef- = his
   - pes-/tes- = her
   - pen-/ten- = our
   - tetn- = your(pl)
   - peu- = their

5. **Number placement**: Numbers follow the noun (e.g., "gafkaf snau" = chickpeas two)

6. **Gender**: Nouns have grammatical gender, affecting articles and possessive forms

7. **Assimilation**: The genitive marker may change form (n-/m-) depending on the following sound
