2.  `D ← LBERT`: If this is `X -> Y` as `D -> LBERT`, then `ADA` becomes `<ADA>`, then applying the rule yields `<ALBERTA>`. This means rules can operate on substrings *within* `<` and `>`.

A safer approach for end-of-string operations is to make the `>` explicit in `X` and `Y` like `X>` -> `Y>`. This implies `>` is replaced by `>`.
