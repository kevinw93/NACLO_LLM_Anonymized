Nouns for people belong to a specific class, marked by prefixes.
-   **Singular (Class 1/1a):** `um-` or `u-`. Examples: `umfana` (the boy), `umngane` (the friend), `ubaba` (father). The singular for "children" (`abantwana`) can be deduced as `umntwana` (the child).
-   **Plural (Class 2):** `aba-`. Examples: `abafana` (the boys), `abangane` (the friends), `abantwana` (the children).

**Verb Conjugation (Subject Concords):**
Verbs agree with the subject through a prefix.
-   `ngi-`: I (e.g., `ngiyafuna` - I want)
-   `u-`: you (sg) or he/she (Class 1 subject) (e.g., `Umfana uyapheka` - The boy is cooking; `Ufuna... na?` - Do you want...?)
-   `si-`: we (e.g., `sifuna` - we want)
-   `ba-`: they (Class 2 subject) (e.g., `Abafana bayahamba` - The boys are going)

**Verb Structure and Tense:**
The general verb structure is `Subject Prefix - (Tense Marker) - Verb Root`.
-   **Present Tense Marker `-ya-`:** This marker is used in the present tense when the verb is not immediately followed by an object or an infinitive. Examples: `ngiyafuna` (I want), `bayahamba` (they are going). When an object or infinitive follows, `-ya-` is omitted: `Sifuna ukuhamba` (We want to go), `Abafana banatha itiye na?` (Are the boys drinking tea?).
-   **Infinitive:** The infinitive "to" form of a verb is created with the prefix `uku-`. Examples: `ukuhamba` (to go), `ukupheka` (to cook), `ukufunda` (to learn).

**Question Formation:**
-   **Yes/No Questions:** Add the particle `na?` to the end of the statement. (e.g., `Abangane bayahamba na?` - Are the friends going?).
-   **Tag Questions:** Add the particle `angithi?` to the end of the statement. This particle seems to be fixed for different subjects. (e.g., `Umngane uyahamba angithi?` - The friend is going, isn't he?).
-   **"Wh-" Questions:**
    -   Words like `njani` (how?) and `ngaphi` (where?) can be prefixed with the subject concord (e.g., `Banjani abantwana?` - How are the children?; `Bangaphi abafana?` - Where are the boys?).
    -   They can also appear after the verb (e.g., `Uthunga njani?` - How do you sew?).
