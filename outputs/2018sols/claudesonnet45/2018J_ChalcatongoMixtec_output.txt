**J3: Explain how Chalcatongo Mixtec words and sentences are structured.**

Based on my analysis:

**Word Structure:**
- Adjectives: simple roots (ñi?ñi "hot", súkú "tall", ndáa "true", etc.)
- Nouns: may have definite article prefix nde- (nduča "the water", ndežu "the food")
- Nominalized adjectives: xa- prefix creates "one who is X" or "the X one" (xasúčí "young person" from súčí "young", xakwíí "the green one" from kwíí "green")
- Possessed nouns (predicate): xa-ROOT-rí pattern for "my X" (xalúlírí "my child" from lúlí "small", xažiirí "my husband")

**Sentence Structure:**

Two copulas:
- kaa: used with adjective predicates (states/properties)
- kúu: used with noun predicates (identity, class membership)

Word order patterns:
1. **Emphatic subject**: Subject kaa/kúu Predicate (when subject is emphasized/contrastive in English)
   - Nduča kaa ñi?ñi = "**The water** is hot"
   - Pedro kaa súkú = "**Pedro** is tall"

2. **Neutral**: Predicate Subject (no copula) (when neither element is emphasized)
   - Ñi?ñi nduča = "The water is hot"
   - Nde?é Maria = "Maria is brave"

3. **Minimal** (subject dropped): kaa/kúu Predicate (when subject is understood/non-contrastive)
   - Kaa ndáa = "**It** is true" (with emphasis)
   - Ndáa = "It is true" (neutral)

The marker "ii" appears in some sentences, possibly as indefinite article or person marker.

Possessive marking differs by syntactic position:
- In predicate: xa-ROOT-rí for "my X"
- In subject: possibly ii + NOUN (needs more data)
