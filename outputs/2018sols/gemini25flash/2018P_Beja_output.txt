    *   Example: `Akra tak rihan` (Strong man I saw) - "I saw a strong man."

**3. Relative Clauses:**
Relative clauses provide additional information about a noun and are formed through distinct morphological and syntactic patterns, depending on whether they are derived from an adjective or a verb.

*   **Adjective-derived Relative Clauses ("that is X"):**
    *   **Formation:** These are formed by adding the suffix `-b` to a simple adjective.
        *   Example: `Akra` (strong) → `Akraab` (that is strong); `Dabalo` (small) → `Dabaloob` (that is small); `Kwati` (happy) → `Kwatiib` (that is happy).

*   **Verb-derived Relative Clauses ("that I X"):**

        *   **Post-nominal:** The suffix `-eeb` is added to the verb stem (e.g., `rihan` → `rihaneeb` 'that I saw'; `akteen` → `akteeneeb` 'that I know').
    *   **Position:**
