**Key Pattern: Reduplication**
By comparing reduplicated and non-reduplicated forms, a pattern emerges.
*   **Reduplicated forms** (`witewiteihi`, `itowotowolo`, `odaladala`, `memehi`, `gobugobuhi`, `Hiduhuduhuna`, `idanedanenehi`, `daodaohi`) are used for:
    1.  **Continuous/Durative Actions:** Actions happening over time (crawling, standing, sitting, stealing).
    2.  **Stative Qualities:** Describing a state of being (swampy, muddy, long, difficult).
    3.  **Plurality:** Often triggered by a plural noun or subject (e.g., `daodaohi` "long legs", `witewiteihi` "are difficult" with plural subject).
*   **Non-reduplicated forms** (`uwitai`, `unagobu`, `pipeuna`, `memena`, `inadao`, `unapeu`, `hipeu`, `imae`) are used for:
    1.  **Punctual Actions:** Single, non-continuous events (fall).
    2.  **Future Changes of State:** (will be dirty, will be long).
    3.  Some stative verbs with singular subjects (`uwitai`, `imae`). This suggests an interaction between aspect and number.
